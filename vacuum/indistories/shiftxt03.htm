I'm standing in front of the bathroom mirror, turning the sink faucet on to soak a bit of toilet paper and wipe away the dried toothpaste splatter on the smooth surface of the looking glass. My reflection says I look like shit. Eyebags, insomniac's skin, crabby countenance. I feel worse than the way I look.

The tiled wall behind me is plastered with images and colors. Paper and the other stuff we couldn't throw away: Promotional postcards with nice pictures , pages from glossy magazines, Foto-me ID shots of us during our giddy dating days , catchy quotes, bumper stickers, the reflections of Luke's huge comic book pin-up collection all staring back at me.

Horror vacui. Fear of empty spaces.

I learned that term back in college at my art studies elective course where Lucas and I first met. He was the mischievous big guy across the aisle making wonderful caricatures of Professor Obinia and showing them to a bunch of us right after class. Always aware of his presence, I never thought he'd acknowledge my existence until Christmas break rolled around and he gave me this flat parcel wrapped in red handmade paper and abaca twine . I opened it and saw a sketchpad full of images of myself in different angles, rendered in pen and ink, the way Lucas saw me that whole semester.

Horror vacui. Fear of empty spaces. Lucas's aversion to blanks. Blank pages, blank walls, heavy silences. Blankity-blank-blank blank. I feel like a TV screen going kaput. The whole picture shrinks into a single white dot before it disappears into total blackness. Lucas hates blanks and now I hate Lucas who likes being in the thick of things and gets easily lured by the next catchy thing. Huge crowds around showroom window TV screens attract him and he always slows down like to be part of a throng gathered round a road mishap. There never was a dull moment with Lucas talking and being in the flow, dishing out advice, fascinating people with his sketchbook doodles. I hate that he no longer tries to impress me.

Breakfasts with Alex, art exhibit openings, who knows what else? I'm not even sure of what I'm jealous of anymore, the attention he gives Alex , or his job happiness and fulfillment that I don't seem to be a part of anymore. Lucas is so full of that shit, he has no more room for me.

Alex fills him with her spotlight and theatrics. I don't wanna know what he fills her with. I need my space to wallow in. Wide open spaces to run to, no clutter and no bad sleep.

There's nothing like the sound of cardboard and paper ripping. Bits of bathroom tile break off and stick to the stubborn tape that has gone yellow with time.

My destruction of the bathroom collage doesn't help me feel better. My apologies to The Invisibles, The Maxx, and Doom Patrol.

I read somewhere that solitude is really filled with other people.

Lucas finally gets home and I'm only halfway done with cleaning up the angry mess I made in the rest of the apartment. Rumpled bedsheets and towels are still on the floor. The do-it-yourself bookcase has to be reassembled and books and magazines are flung on a messy heap on the bare mattress. The Japanese dividers lie collapsed so the apartment's partitions for sleeping and eating have now merged into one whole dump.

"That time of the month again?" the sexist asks.

I ignore him completely, walk to the kitchen sink and start washing a pile of crockery and greasy pans even though I know it's his turn to do them.

"It's Alex isn't it?" he starts

Well, Du-uh?!! I won't even start asking about her.

"Long talks, that's all I ever have with her, Glen."

I think of my long talk and walk with Makie. - there definitely were sparks there. But that's nothing compared to what fireworks I imagine are going on between Alex and Luke. The thought of them just hitting it off with animated conversation and all that shared laughter makes me just as jealous as the thought of catching them getting physical.

My hands briskly scrub the insides of a coffeepot until the soap suds come out of the spout . I hate washing dishes, likewise this sound of steel wool on stoneware. Lucas tries to take over and picks up a soft sponge to wipe the frying pan I'm doing next. He moves me out of the way of the sink then tries to concentrate on the task but I can see his hands all fidgety.

The lump in my throat gets bigger and the silence becomes heavy till a small, wavering voice forces its way out of me.

"I feel excluded Lucas. Why do you exclude me?"

"I'm sorry if you felt out of place at school Glen. Is that why you were so weird at the canteen?"

"I was what? -"

"Maybe I haven't given you much attention lately. You know I can't be around you all the time."

"I, want you to NOT be there all the time so I can have someone to run to. I know you have your thing and I have mine. But we used to share something."

"Your job is getting to you-" He starts again

"I don't think it's just that, Lucas. What about those weekends you went off and left me."

"You weren't interested in my parties and outings. You told me to go ahead."

"In case you didn't notice, I was tired and feeling down. Why should I drag you into my misery?"

"Ask yourself that now, Glenda."

Silence.

"So is this it, huh? The part in movies and books where the doomed pair realizes they're drifting apart and shit like that?" I say.

Heavier silence.

"I'm sorry I'm not so into us anymore." Lucas says at last.

My face begins to grow really hot and I look away as my insides get into meltdown mode.

Lucas and I. We move in different spaces now.

I just lost the biggest depositor in my resentment memory bank and I'm only halfway done dealing with it. I've been through stress, appetite loss, nausea, slight alcoholism, the usual heavy heartbreak shit and the all those clichés that really make you feel that a part of you died.

I wouldn't remember anything else from that day we broke up except that in the midst of our quasi-confrontation, the buzzer rang and Lucas beat me to the door. Talk about perfect timing. Makie was just in the neighborhood having dinner with a friend at some nearby sisig place and he was wondering if he could pick me up on the way to work. Lucas turned around and took one long sad look at me. I shook my head so he told Makie that I wasn't feeling well. The door closed and I heard Makie start his car. Then I took a walk by myself to nowhere while Lucas packed his bags.    >>>
