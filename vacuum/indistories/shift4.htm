It's been months since the break-up. After his brief appearance that fateful day, Mark Macasaet asked me if Lucas was my brother. I said he used to be my live-in boyfriend. Makie has been sort of avoiding me since then. I still catch him averting his gaze from me every now and then.

It's funny how we both knew nothing about each other's personal lives. Tina says Makie has this long time, long-distance girlfriend that he's feeling frustrated about. The sexual tension between us is on hold. I'm swearing off relationships for a while to fix the one I have with myself. The day-mares still bug me. I now try to keep them at bay with newly learned meditation exercises and consultations with that psychic Jesuit Nilo Veratta.

I have one more week to go till this graveyard shift is over and now I'm getting ready to move out of the Quezon City flat and get a life. I peeled off Lucas's glow-in-the-dark stars from the ceiling above what's now my own bed. I gave most of them away to these office people and stuck three of the smallest ones above the window frame near my desk.

Wasn't it Oscar Wilde who once said we're all living in the gutter but some of us look up at the stars.
