A star fell on my face when I was lying in bed this morning. It was a plastic, glow-in-the-dark one, with its adhesive all worn out, a chunk of some stellar arrangement Lucas made on our ceiling.

What startled me more than the quick jab on my cheekbone was my sudden inability to raise my arm and touch my smarting skin. I tried to shake off what little sleep I had but my head felt like it weighed a hundred pounds. Then I rubbed my right arm against the sheet (all soft and smooth from years of washing) but the sight of that very arm lying still on the faded blue gingham defied all sensations of movement and texture. >>>
