Things haven't been too easy for me ever since I got into Graveyard Shift at Towerbank and it's only been 3 weeks. The hours aren't so vile: 10 pm to 6 am instead of 9-5 at thrice the pay. I did expect some difficulty in sleeping schedules since I, for a fact, never doze during the day. The lights should always be turned off every time I sleep. A sliver of sun from a doorway crack distracts me and I can never dig a lifestyle requiring cute little night lights plugged into wall sockets. I expected my biological clock to go bonkers these first few days but what I didn't expect were the nightmares.

Technically I shouldn't call them nightmares because they don't happen when I sleep at night. They only happen when I sleep during the day. (That is why I never practiced siesta as a kid. I would always run off by myself when my playmates had to take their afternoon naps.) Besides, I don't think I should classify those paralyzing episodes as dreams.

Makie, a guy from work, seems to comprehend what I'm going through. He gets these falling sensations in his sleep and he wakes up all freaked out, grasping his pillow and kicking the sheets. According to him, it's a body-mind thing. He suggests that what I'm going through might lead to astral projection or some funky out-of-body experience that I really don't need in my life right now. What I need right now is a shot of vodka, or melatonin or something.

It's 8:30 and Lucas isn't home yet. Here I am, sitting by his drawing board, waiting for Godot and staring at his stuff. Taped on the frame of his overhead lamp is a clipped classified ad that had us in hysterics two weeks ago. It says Wanted: Stripper. (A local paper was in need of a new comic strip series and he applied and showed them his work.)
Crumpled balls of paper, the chaff of his thoughts, accumulate on the floor where stacks of sketchbooks and piles of assorted paper rise. Lucas's brightest ideas are in these sheets of cartoons: his latest project, a series called The Friendly Aswangs.

He did his best work at night filling neat little cels with pen and ink for a separate animation project. Sometimes he would be so absorbed, he would forget I was there, waiting for him to join me in bed.

Funny how he always throws in his own physical features in the details of the characters. I see the shape of his eyes on a duwende wearing inline skates, his stubborn cowlick on a muscled tikbalang, and his thick brows on a tobacco-puffing kapre. He used to draw caricatures of me as well….although I don't think there's anything resembling me in this new stack. The only female character in this series is this mermaid with corkscrew curls and enormous eyes behind thick-framed glasses. Nothing like me at all. The phone rings and my train of thought is derailed.
