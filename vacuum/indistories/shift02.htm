I know this sounds sappy but when I was small, I imagined that the chirping of crickets was the sound of the stars. We had this big duhat tree outside mama's window in Las Piñas and that was where the insects resided, their cosmic hiss my constant source of solace. I heard a faint version of the same sound when I moved in here during college but I never get to hear them anymore since I started working. Their sound has been replaced by this harsh hiss of reality, which sounds like a punctured balloon's feeble gasp before it gets all shriveled up on the floor.

As soon as I step out of our apartment to go do my job, everything becomes mechanical. I take a sputtering tricycle to the main road, board a bus to Makati and walk down a block of buildings.

Between the rooftops I catch a glimpse of the sepia sky. Tonight's moon looks flat, you could almost peel it off with your fingernails. Two curious, necktie-clad passersby wonder what I'm staring at and tilt their heads. I walk past them and enter the spanking steel and glass facade of the Tower. The guard nods his head as I stride to the elevator to get to my floor where I punch in my identification code, swipe a card, don headphones, face the computer and begin my shift.

My job is in international customer service. I handle all the calls of clients who use their credit cards abroad. I make dollar transfers for their fat bank account funds on Eastern Standard Time. Truly thrilling. Welcome to my exciting world.

Actually, this is my second time to work for a bank. The first time was in a more conservative bank that fired staffers who got pregnant out of wedlock, had a really prissy dresscode, and hired background investigators that traced where you lived. Of course they got on my case for living in with Lucas. Relatively speaking, the graveyard shift is a much better environment. At least I don't have to deal with rush hour traffic jams and the nine to five culture of buying stuff from officemates who make you pay for unwanted fake designer shirts in a "two gives" installment plan. At least I'm not part of that bunch of cup-noodle slurping, stocking-clad office girls who flock to the ladies room with their floral printed vanity kits for their daily ritual of tooth brushing and gossip. (Now that was weird. One time, from the corner of the washroom, I saw this girl literally foaming at the mouth talking about stapler ownership rights violations and well, that culprit she was bashing turned out to be me.) The only reason I'm working in a bank again is the money: the carrot on a stick that keeps me in this race. What drives me is thought that when I've finally saved enough cash, I'd be able to quit all this and put up my own biz, possibly in video editing.

Some people doing graveyard are in it for short-term reasons as well. Dong, the guy two cubicles away is saving up for his dream wedding. Tina is the insomniac who strives hard to complete the education plan for her kid. Patty is the well-dressed, bar hopping nine-to-fiver who's going back to her old shift as soon as she pays her credit card dues. Marko Macasaet, or Makie for short, sits in the cubicle next to mine. He's working to pay off his car loan and he has two more years to go.

Lanky, boyish, and bespectacled Makie, has been extra nice to me. He brought me to my doorstep two days ago in that car that he still slaves away for. He lives in the South but he was going my direction that morning to pick up his mother's X-Ray results at the Veterans Hospital.

It was in his car that I got to tell him about my sleeping troubles (he brought it up by asking me why I always looked like I needed sleep). The conversation drifted to a whole bunch of other topics like Tina's horrible haircut, rants about the drudgery of work, the new PJ Harvey CD , and when and where to watch experimental films for free. I never mentioned Lucas to him that morning. It was such a short drive.

***


It's six AM now and faint daylight is peeking through the tinted window's vertical blinds. Ergonomic chairs can only do so much for my lower back which aches like hell after hours on my ass. My eyes feel like a peeled grape and I'm on the verge of collapsing.

"Hey you!" Makie's head pops up from the divider. "Wanna grab some grub at the doughnut shop on Jupiter ?" His has a nice wide smile with even teeth.

"Sorry, Makie but I really have to -"

"No buts here, lady."

I'm not really in my element for small talk and I get this sudden compulsion to brush my teeth.

"Makie, I'm not really up to it, okay?" I hope he doesn't hear my stomach grumbling. He pulls a long face and bargains.

"How about some taho then?"

"I have a friend who took up Food Technology and she says that that stuff has plaster of Paris."

"Aww, come on. There's much worse in the other stuff we eat in the canteen. Okay, if it kills you, funeral expenses are on me."

"Hmmm…Okay, done deal." How could I resist when he feigns disappointment so badly?

We step outside just before the city's hustle bustle unfolds. Makie forgets about his precious car in the basement as we walk to Ayala Avenue. The air is cool and cleared of yesterday's smog. The few sidewalk vendors are chatty and not too stressed yet. We see the paperboy laying out his wares while the faint smell of kerosene tells us that the bananacue vendor is there. Pretty soon we find our regular taho vendor and make his day. Makie chugs taho faster than it takes the man to dish it out. I watch as the vendor scoops equal amounts of the white glop into our cups, tops it with tapioca and stirs in the syrup that coats my throat with sweetness and warmth.

"Taho is truly an amazing food product." Makie says after three straight servings of it.

"Ummmh -huh?" I say with raised brows.

"I mean, this is the only kind of meal that looks the same before and after the process of digestion."

And so we laugh the rest of the way until we reach the bus stop and it 's time for me to head home.



***

At home, in our room, I am welcomed by the sight of Lucas sprawled on the sheets, hogging the bed as usual. For a while, I watch him in deep slumber, his mouth parted open and his knuckles curled against the sheet. Stroking his chin and feeling his goatee stubble against my palm, I remember how I could never really fall asleep beside him during our first few nights together. I would be fully attuned to his breathing, aware of that open mouth and his loosening grip. How I envy these men who fall asleep fast (ah yes, the same men who climax fast, get hungry fast and eat even faster.) And yet, I miss the snoring that wakes me up in the middle of the night.

I kick off my loafers, peel off my silk socks, blazer, blouse and slacks to don an oversized shirt. Then I gently nudge my bed-hog to move over to his side of the mattress. Lucas flutters his eyes open, rubs the sleep from them then sits up. He kisses me then helps tuck me in while I slip underneath the covers which are still warm from his body. My first few seconds in bed are also his last.

***
I must have slept for 5 hours and now I'm in that now familiar stage between sleep and the waking world. Consciousness sets in as the stupid phone rings. But my body, once more, refuses to cooperate. Do not panic, I tell my frozen self. So I breathe deeply for a few seconds and start to kick the air with my right leg. At the fifth attempt it moves with a surprising suddenness. I stand up and walk across the room to pick up the phone. The caller has hung up. It might be Lucas, so I dial his school number but the line is busy. Then I pace up and down and around the room . I can fight this. I can control this. I pick up the phone and redial. Still busy. I feel trapped and confused.

Not having anything better to do, I get dressed for work and go out to kill time in a shopping mall. I have more than enough time to see three whole movies but what would happen if I fell asleep and got stuck there forever?

So I decide to take a cab to Manila and surprise Lucas with a deli submarine sandwich from the mall.



***


When I get to the school and look for Lucas, the guard asks me who I am and I tell him I'm Mr. Lucas Santiago's, fiancee (even though I 'm not, but it sounds more official than just 'girlfriend'). The guard's monobrow almost disappears into his blue cap. Why is he giving me this weird look?

Once in, I check the classrooms and the faculty lounge but Lucas isn't there. Hoping that he hasn't eaten yet, I head off in the direction of the canteen.

I see the familiar back of Lucas's head as he sits near some stone tables laughing his head off with someone. I walk a few steps closer to get a full view of this person that amuses him so much. It is a woman who I could mistake for one of the older students if she was in a blue and white uniform like these droves of prepubescent girls around me .

The woman looks familiar. Now where have I seen that long curly hair and the thick rimmed glasses that frame her big, round eyes?

Suddenly, a throng of boisterous kids nearly mows me down . A tall girl comes from behind me and yells a shrill . "Hiyeeeee Miss Cruuuuzadas!!"

Miss Ringlets waves with much gusto from her stone bench and I bite my lip. Alex Cruzadas. The theater teacher. The Mermaid inspiration. Oh, what I'd give to run a pitchfork through that fabulous hair.

I take in some air and do some yelling myself.

"Sir Santieeeeeeagoh!"

Lucas turns around. Why am I not surprised by that surprised look on his face?

"Glenda!" he exclaims as he stands up. "What? - Why?-"

I dangle the deli paperbag, and toss it in his direction. He catches it neatly, plunks it on the table and walks towards me. Then he clasps my hand and drags me to introduce me to the little mermaid with the big hair.

"This is-"

"Alex, the theater teacher. Yes, I 've heard so much about you." I say with a fake smile. She smiles back and stretches out her hand.

"Alex, this is Glenda." Lucas says and pulls me closer. Frankly, I don't think I can remember feeling more distant to him than I do right now as he wraps his arm around my waist.

 

***


This has to be the worst meal in my entire life. What's wrong with this picture? Me, me, me. At the school lunch counter, Miss Mermaid giggles at Lucas and his stupid jokes. Jokes I've heard since grade school, has she not heard these punch lines before? Way too much laughter from her makes me uncomfortable. Am I imagining it, or has some sort of chemistry been unfolding behind my back?

Now they talk about art exhibits, tabla drums, fellow teachers, favorite students, and more people and things I don't know about. What have I got to talk about from my life? Work and how crappy it is? I just realized I don't have a life outside Lucas, and work and occasional trips to the mall. So many thoughts running through my mind and I can't blurt them out. How convenient for Lucas, she's just right here.

"Remember that skinhead guy in black at Starbucks, Tuesday morning?" Alex asks him.

Starfuck! Luke never brought me to Starbucks.

"Starbucks! Where the trendy hang out and make believe they like real coffee? I hear that place smacks of pretension." Of course I just have to say that.

"Well, it isn't so bad..." she replies, waving a drinking straw between her fingers like it was a cigarette. "Piped in Fitzgerald, nice interiors, It's actually pretty cozy."

Cozy breakfasts at Starbucks. Right before class!

"I'm sure it's pretty. Pretty cozy." I mutter. A cozy for place for poseurs. Lucas seems preoccupied with shaking the ice in his yellow paper cup. Maybe he realizes that he never once took me out to breakfast anywhere.

"Well, anyways," Alex goes on, and looks at Lucas as well. "That guy was the son of National artist Bernardino Pepua."

So what, name dropper?

"Really?" Lucas says with genuine interest. "He was my favorite artist as a kid. We went on a field trip to one of the few galleries in Manila and I fell in love with his "Sunog" series." How come I never knew that part of his life?

"Who's your favorite artist, Glenda?" Alex asks generously. Alabaster skin, red lips, big eyes with matching sweeping lashes. If I shaved off her crowning glory she'd still look good.

"Oh Glen likes Gustav Klimt's - " Lucas starts.

"I hate Gustav Klimt!" Awkward silence. A shrill siren pierces it. Lucas and Alex are saved by the bell. I head for home and think about calling in sick at work. >>>
