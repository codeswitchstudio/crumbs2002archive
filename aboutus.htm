"Bah, let's not describe our site. It's like writing the word 'notes' on a piece of paper."
- freelance lover #1


Indi & Adam used to work for this site for teens where they grew to love editing and designing stories for the web. After several months of doing that (or at least, trying to do that), they had to pack up and find greener pastures with feelings of unfulfilled not-for-the-diabetic eye-candy dreams in their hearts.

Fortunately, their brush with capitalist dotCOM ventures left them rather unscathed. Whatever dreams they had were still packed inside cardboard boxes lined with bubble-wrap and four-fold sealed with masking tape. As Freelancers, they eventually used their free time to fulfill their dreams of postcard prose with pop-artstravaganzic garments in CRUMBS!!

CRUMBS!! (which is still -and always will be- a work in progress) was borne out of late night conversations between Indi and Adam. Several phone conversations, line art scanning sessions, several rotations of B&S records, and about thirty-five daily email exchanges later, Freelance Lovers' CRUMBS!! was online. The first section uploaded was MAGNETIC PROSE ON EYE-CANDY HYPERLINK. Some friends thought it was rather clever and pretty, so they decided to do more things with the site.

It turns out that both their hard drives were brimming with stories (mostly set in the smoggy Third World Metro where they manage to thrive) as well as drawings, photographs, and ideas, which they eventually tweaked in Photoshop, laid out in Dreamweaver, and threaded with a bunch of shareware javascripts. And so in a bit of a daze, they declared most of their stuff actually fit for public consumption.

"... I got his books, though." she said with a laugh.

An early morning conversation, with both of us rather drunk: her with sleepiness, and me with beer.

'Hehe! I do that sort of thing, too. We're not a couple anymore, but that's alright, as I have her books! Hehehehe!!' I said with a rather energetic laugh. Beer has that effect on me. 'I wonder if all of your books come from your exes..."

"Bah!! I'm a Freelance Lover, dammit!!" she replied, and through the phone lines, I saw her smile.
We both laughed like idiots. I think it was the beer. On my part, at least.

- Jules Feiffer
