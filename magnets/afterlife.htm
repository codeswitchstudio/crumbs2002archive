I dreamt of her the other night. She was wearing this black lacy knitted dress (that hugged her body quite nicely). Between the gaps in the black dress, I got glimpses of her pale flesh. I was wearing a pair of green trousers, and a black shirt.

We went inside this room where the walls were decorated with pink curtains, the wallpaper had this floral pattern, it, too, in pink. She was with these girls, one of them from old my high school, and the other one was Camryn Manheim.

In my head in the dream, I knew that there was a funeral going on. A girl's funeral, but for some strange reason, it felt like it was someone's debutante ball. It had that feeling throughout: the rooms, the colors, the dresses, the smells...

I took her hand and I kissed it, a light peck. I looked up at her and we both smiled.

And then, we started to dance. It was a slow sort of dance, with my head cradled on her shoulder (as she was taller than I was), and with her head cradled over mine (she had to stoop a bit). Hands clasped together, we swayed slowly to the music (which I forgot, for some reason). I could smell the faint scent of oranges as we danced. Cool oranges in a temperate summer day. That sort of smell. We danced for quite a while.

We kissed, and that's when I woke up.

"If you ever die before me, ask if you could bring a friend," I remember thinking. I made a mental note, to tell her that next time we meet.

"It was a sweet dream," I thought, before drifting back to an uneventful sleep.

-Juli
