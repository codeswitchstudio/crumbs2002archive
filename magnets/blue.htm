Whenever one of us felt blue, (him usually in between checking endless stacks of his students papers, me usually in between jobs or relationships.) we would find some time to meet and rant about our lives over brewed coffee and an oatmeal cookie.

Lance was fond of The Azure, this cozy cafe near the Makati Med which had this whole lighthouse theme to it; genuine anchors and portholes, life savers threaded by thick ropes, salt and paper shakers in the form of lighthouses, the works. And we'd sit there by the coffeeshop window watching the rush hour yuppies on the street. Then we'd just talk for hours.

Lance would usually start by sharing his exuberance for whatever book it was in which he chose to bury his nose in: Anne Carson's mindfucking essays on Eros, William Carlos Williams' biography, Mark Strand's compelling poetry.

While I'd rave about Murakami, Chungking Express, or comic books, he'd initiate a very scholarly discussion on love, ethics, and metaphysics (which I'd usually interrupt with a wisecrack or a bit of lame gossip I'd hear about people in circles we shared.)

A good coffee break and a great exchange of stuff that feeds our heads.

Then it would be dark outside and we'd look for a cigarette vendor by one of the lamp posts . After a stick of nicotine, we'd part ways: him back to checking his papers, me back to my everyday world, a sense of wonder regained.

-Saffron
