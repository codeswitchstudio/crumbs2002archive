I have this memory from childhood, of a red unblinking eye in the dark. The radio's eye, actually. Those things that tell us that the radio's turned on and playing (as if the music coming out of the speakers isn't enough indication). Every night, my parents would turn down the lights and burn some sandalwood incense and smoke some dope as they listened to Cohen, his voice reverberating across the room, singing: 'Suzanne takes you down... to her place near the river... you can hear the boats go by... you can spend the night beside her... and you know she's half-crazy... but that's why you wanted to be there... '

As my eyes adjusted to the darkness, I would see faint outlines of the Hare Krishna posters on the walls of their room, orange and yellow prints against red-varnished wooden panels. The air inside the room would be thick with the smell of dope and sandalwood, which always did make me feel calm. My mom and dad would sing with Cohen, almost whispering, like they were saying a prayer. Maybe they were.

In the cozy folds of the pillows and blankets, I would pretend to be asleep, just so I could hear them sing, an accidental lullabye for me. - Juli
