For some reason, she wore this bandana over her head, her hair cut short like a boy's. It suited her, the haircut, and everytime I'd see her wearing that bandana, covering her hair, I'd always ask myself "Why?". She also wore these dark shades (that she got for about 20 pesos from a vendor below the MRT), its frame violet.

Her skin has a healthy yellow tinge to it, pale yellow, like a lemon seen through a white silk-screen frame. And her lips are pink, like cotton candy and Bazooka Joe's. And one thing about her (and I just thought of this), is that she never wears make-up. No lipstick, no mascara, no foundations blahblahblah.

A breath of fresh air, actually.

She was lying down in front of me, her back to the carpet, her head propped up by a pillow. I was lying on my belly, my upper torso propped up by my elbows. Arranged like yin and yang, like the Piscean twin fish, like sixty-nine.

Our noses were touching each other, nostrils to nostrils. "This must be how the Eskimos French-kiss," I told her, rubbing my nose against hers.

What she exhaled, I inhaled.

"Hehehe. It smells like beer!" she said with a bit of a laugh. And yeah, it did smell of beer. It was warm and musky and gamey. A mix of beer and femininity. It was pretty intoxicating. More intoxicating than the beer, even.

That close to her, I could see tiny blood vessels on her cheeks. "... like pink delicate spider webs..." I remember reading once, from one of her stories.

My eyes were directly over her lips, and at the back of my head, I was thinking "I wonder how her lips would feel like, against mine? Soft, even pillowy, perhaps?"

Almost by reflex (an urge, a jolt in my brain), I moved up to kiss her. A light peck on the lips.

I pulled away (the contact was only for a second or two, no more) and looked at her. Her skin now had this yellowish-pinkish tinge to it (pink lemonade!!) from the beer, her lips still Bazooka Joe's-pink (soft and sweet and pink as cotton candy!!). No lipstick, no mascara, no foundations blahblahblah.

She was the most beautiful sight I had ever seen. And she was smiling.

We breezed through the night in a bit of a daze.

The morning after, clearing the table, fluffing the pillows a bit, I found her bandana on one of the couches in the living room, and her dark shades on one chair (where she put her down her blue bag).

I smiled when I saw them, for some strange reason. Reminded me of her, I guess.

I made a mental note to give these back to her, next week, maybe? Maybe.
