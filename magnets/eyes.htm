"What do you see?" she asked me as we stared into each other's eyes.

Nosetip to nosetip, our eyes about half an inch from each other. That close, her eyes blurred into one orb, a cyclopean mirror, a dark-brown island in a sea of white...

I stared at her eyes (hers were rounder than usual, like oblong plates, like langue de chat), and I remember swimming around, and drowning inside.

"Your eyes are the saddest eyes I've ever seen..." she told me. And I believed her. At that point, I'd believe anything and everything she said. "Uh-huh," I hummed my acknowledgement.

"What do you see?" she asked me again. And for good measure, "What are you thinking of?"

I was still staring when I answered "You..."
