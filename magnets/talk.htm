Tired and drenched from the rain, Elise and I sat on the steps and just talked about things.

She tells me of the time she gave birth to Leif and Enrico wasn't there by her side. He was busy finishing his first major film. At the hospital, she says, everyone hated her for being a mess and coming in at the last minute, with the baby almost out. Her parents didn't approve of a half-Oriental baby either.

For two months she stayed at the commune, wearing herself out over the child. Soon enough, she was overcome by the strain of getting up in the middle of the night, feeding the baby, and changing his diapers. (How could she concentrate on sculptures stained-glass art while the baby bawled for milk and attention?)

It was fall when Enrico finally finished his film, Elise told him to take the kid and be off. She thought she wanted to see the last of him and whatever they shared together. So he drove off in his strange jeep, their little pink bundle of a boy in the front seat.

Days later the euphoria of her regained freedom turned to depression.

It took one phone call to change everything. "Want to go to with me to the Philippines?" Enrico said over the line. "Ja!!!" she said without hesitation.

Leif was 2 when his folks got married in the highlands. They had 12 sandwiches and rice wine for the reception. Elise's heart hasn't really left the Third World ever since.

-Circe
