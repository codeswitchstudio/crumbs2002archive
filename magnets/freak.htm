I had this bisexual Brazilian friend back in high school. His name was Bruno, and he had the biggest muscles in the whole campus. He was pretty strong, like a gorilla. One time, we were at another friend's house, this Vietnamese guy named John. They lived in a condominium in Greenhills (right beside the mall and the school), and on the top floor, near the swimming pool, was this private gym. Like most gyms, they had one of those things where you pull some weights using a bar hanging on a length of steel cable running along pulleys.

For fun, I tried to pull the bar down to my chest, about five weights on it. No go. I was dangling like a monkey, the bar only budged an inch or two. John and another friend of mine, Angelo, helped me out, pulling the bar down with me. Still no go. The bar only budged three inches.

Then along came Bruno. He sat down on the seat and pulled the bar down to his chest. And he made it stay there for half a minute. He was pretty strong.

He was pretty good with the guitar, too. He taught me how to play samba (an Astrud Gilberto song comes to mind. Forgot the title, though), and some daft chord progressions and scales. Learned a lot from that guy.

Bruno and I used to hang out by the staircase near our classrooms (he wasn't a classmate, but we were on the same level), near the girls' toilet. We sat on the steps and just talked about things that interested the both of us: guitar-playing, music, this girl named Michelle...

One day, we were talking about Yngwee Malmsteen, when this girl named Ethel (a classmate of his) butted in, saying "Is it really that great, playing the guitar?"

My train of thought a bit derailed, I said the first thing that came to my mind:

"Only when I pee."

Ethel looked at me like I was some kind of freak.

When I graduated from high school, they gave me this scholarship on Computer Programming, sponsored by Ethel's father, who owns this big computer supply place that supplied the computers in our school. I never did take that scholarship. Wasn't that interested in computers. Not that much.

As for Bruno, last I heard from him, he's taking Computer Engineering in some business school a few streets from our place.

Haven't talked to him in a while, now. But I still play that Astrud song, every once in a while. Oiling the gears a bit, I guess.
