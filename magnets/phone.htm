I met Lily back in high school, on our Senior year. She was thin and dark, and just about my height. She came from Singapore, her family moved back to the Philippines to take care of her dying grandmother's home and assets and whatnot.

I fell madly in love with her on a September morning. It was a Saturday, on our field trip to one of those provinces four to five hours from Manila proper. The sun was up and it was pretty much cooking us with our own sweat. I was hanging about with this kid (who'd later turn out to be her younger brother) named Abraham. A pretty great kid, smart for his age and all that. Liked him, first time I met him. He was that sort of guy.

I saw Lily walking towards me (actually, towards Abraham), the pavement beneath her feet glowing, like it was light itself. It made these strange reflections on her face, light form below. It was a nice effect. Time stopped for me, that moment.

The months after that went by like water down the sinkhole, fast and liquid. We talked on the phone like anything, discussing love and religion and philosophy and books and whatnot. The things that I used to do to impress girls.

One night, we were talking about pendulums, when she shifted the topic to this guy named Christopher. He was a schoolmate (we were even in the same level)and a neighbor, and I knew him pretty well, being friends since we were Freshmen. I went along with the ride, with me sharing some dull anecdotes about the guy.

And then, all of a sudden, she said "I have a crush on him."

I was absolutely quiet for about half a minute.

Haven't talked to her since, and that conversation is about six years old, now. I got a letter from her recently. She's in Canada right now.

She didn't even phone me when before she left. Wouldn't really have helped, but well, you know.

I still think about her, every once in a while.

-Juli
