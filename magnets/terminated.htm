When I was eleven years old, I happened upon my birth certificate. It was the original, with the embossed seal of St. Luke's Hospital on it, a blister on the paper. I was looking for my uncle's old Lego pieces, digging through the old cockroach-eaten cardboard boxes full of books and old toys and folders chock-full of paper.

I found the birth certificate between the pages of my old books from kindergarten (crayola-moustaches drawn over the photographs). My name was there (of course), along with other personal information that were up-to-date, a few minutes after I was born.

My eyes drifted down towards FETAL DEATHS. It said 01 in typewritten numerals. I asked my mother about it.

"You see," she started slowly. "there was a kid before you. We didn't have enough money, then, so we had to have it... aborted.

"It was depressing (i can imagine). I know you have no idea how it's like, but...

"Your father and I vowed that the next time a baby comes, we'd be prepared. Your dad worked his ass off, in concerts, in side-projects, in his boardwork... the money piled up like anything.

"And then, when we had you, we had enough money. And we were happy." and with that she went back to painting the ceiling.

And right there, breathing the stale smell of wet paint, I believed her, then.

Today, when I think about it, knowing what I know, I think my mom was lying.

Every once in a while, I think about the baby that came before me, who it could've been. I sometimes pretend that I was that baby. I'd think how I thought the baby would think, if I was it.

But the thing is, I'm male. I always did think that the lost baby was a girl.

-Juli
