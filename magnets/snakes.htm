Snake liquor. That has got to be the strangest thing I ever drank. Lorca's uncle said he got it from Vietnam and he dared us to try it after dinner at their place.

It shouldn't be too different from those worms in tequila, I thought. Besides, I was feeling particularly adventurous that day so I had no second thoughts until Lorca lugged the huge carafe from the dirty kitchen

Snake liquor, indeed it was. There were four bloated snakes coiled neatly and snugly, head to tail, forming a floating wall that eroded inside that bottle. Sheesh. I could still see their sharp teeth in the wine (which was kind of hazy in some parts so that it was hard to figure where each snake ended and the clear, yellowish alcohol began.)

Memories of bottled specimen samples in bio lab. Formalin-pickled fetuses and what-not in jars right up there on dusty high shelves with the stuffed cats with their empty eyes and their rotting teeth.

Ugh. Lorca slammed two shot glasses on the wooden table and poured the stuff on.

Here's a toast to Adam and Eve and gardens of earthly delights!

I closed my eyes, winced, and lifted the shot glass to my lips. The stuff tasted like gin, only a bit stronger, with an aftertaste so slimy and organic, it made me want to throw up.

Later, Lorca's uncle said the snake liquor was some sort of aphrodisiac: recommended for older men with young wives. I guess it was an acquired taste.
-Circe
