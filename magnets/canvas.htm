I like old shoes.

All my pairs are second-hand (or maybe second-foot?), either passed down on to me from an older relative, or borrowed from a friend, and never returned. Old pairs have a certain charm to them, a certain feel. That comfortable fit, like your lover's hand in yours. It feels at home, like an old friend from high school, your drinking buddy for six years (before he left the country for greener pastures in the Colonies).

I'd wear these shoes until they break down on me. Some of them'd fall apart after a few weeks, most after a few months (there was this one time when this black pair of adidas trainers fell apart when i was walking to my place. i walked two kilometers in my socks)

I got my third pair of Chuck Taylor's from Joel. It was blue (or at least, it used to be blue -when i used it, it had this yellowish tinge to it,) and it had that wear-and-tear look from at least five years of constant use. I found it in his cabinet during one of our Mortal Kombat night marathons, and I fell in love with it instantly. I actually stole it from him, got it without him knowing.

When he saw me wearing them in school, he just shrugged and said "It's all yours, if you buy me lunch.". I bought him a plate of porkchop and rice from the school canteen, and he gave me his Chuck Taylor's.

It fell apart in the span of two years of steady rugged use. It went through hell and high water, literally: wading though knee-high floods and rivers, stepping on hot coals from last night's fire, climbed mountains with them, kicked several pieces of gravel and rock with them. I even wrote poems in the white rubber, drawing band logos and such.

The airholes in the sides were the first ones that gave on me, the metal rings falling from their places. And then the airholes came undone, turned into two slits that ran from the pads of my feet to the first joint of my big toes. The soles followed, coming apart strip by strip. The canvas started fading, too.

People kept telling me to buy a new pair. And not just friends, mind. Even strangers came up to me, saying "Buy a new pair, iho. Don't your parents have money to buy you a new pair of shoes?" I'd give them dirty snobbish looks at that, wrinkling my nose.

Still, I used them. I walked around school in them, without socks, even, so the pink flesh of my feet peeked from the holes and tears. Seen everywhere with them, they became extensions of me. When you say "Siddharta", images of black uncombed hair, sad eyes, a black guitar, and a rugged pair of Chuck Taylor's come to mind. Like Elvis' sideburns, I guess. Those Chuck Taylor's were the sideburns to my Elvis. I even have Kodak prints of me wearing those bastards.

Well-worn out, well-used, I finally turned them in for a pair of black and white Reeboks from Marc. He used it for about a year or two. I used it for three years, before the soles gave out on me in a Saturday. -Juli
