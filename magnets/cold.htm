cold lemon grass tea. that's what i remember. cold lemon grass tea. served in one of those glazed and deliberately deformed brown terra-cotta mugs.

somebody somewhere (i forget) already wrote about what the color green would taste like. well, for me, this was the thing. this lemon-grass tea. if its taste were music, it would have the high notes of a flute, not piercing but crisp and clear and flowing. lemon grass tea with a twist of lime on a hot sticky summer noon makes me think of forest -glade and shades and rustling leaves speckled with the sun; things and days of a more innocent time: fresh and not quite ripe (its flavor neither bursting or luscious yet so exquisitely distinct). a play of sensations on my tongue.

afternoon tea with the ballerda sisters was always an event during those years. those two were big on tea-time and throwing these get-togethers. i might not remember our exact conversations on art and life and the bits of gossip about this or that person anymore. but what i would always remember would be that cold lemon grass tea.

i always meant to brew some myself.

but it wouldn't be the same.

-Circe
