Sixty-seven. I count those little perfect circles, all randomly embossed. Twenty-nine on the right one, thirty-eight on the left. His brown arms were otherwise smooth, and hairless like a baby's.

Before they went off to the pharmacy to get syringe needles for the tattoos, I heard Elise talking about the skin as a canvas, as a record of memories for life passages and pain.

I feel like a sheltered schoolgirl with a silly milk moustache when I'm with him and Elise. They've shared this life-on-the-edge sort of thing. A rehab, trances, a terminated pregnancy, running away to strange lands.....

Now he tells me he never once flinched when he put out those cigarettes on his own skin. The drugs were that strong, he says. His gaze, so unwavering, grows soft with each swig of San Miguel. I look away and awkwardly poke the crackling fire with a dew-damp twig. I never thought, there and then, that I, of all people, would be causing this so-called world-weary survivor, the least bit of pain. -Circe
