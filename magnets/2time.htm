I have this neighbor named Marc who lives in a small two-storey house on the street right beside ours. His brother is a local poet, award-winner and all that. Pretty much a house- hold name of sorts, if you gravitate on those circles.

His brother is about eight years older than Marc is. He's in Kentucky, nowadays, teaching English to Caucasians.

Marc had this girlfriend named Diana. The girl was pretty enough, in a wallflower sort of way. She wore her hair as a short bob, went down to her shoulders. I used to see her often enough, when I passed by Marc's place. We'd just nod and smile at each other. We didn't talk too much, and when we did, it was mostly about some girl from her school named Monica, who, in Diana's words, had 'the biggest crush' on me. She had a sweet voice, I remember.

Marc and Diana had been a couple since we were Juniors, which was about five years ago. They loved each other about as much as lovers can love one another. I even accompanied Marc around a week before their first year anniversary to buy a pair of gold rings for the two of them.

Three months ago, Diana found out (through Marc's mobile) that Marc had another girl. Her name's Odie (yeah, like the dog), and, according to Marc, she likes wathcing soft-core porn flicks in sticky-floored theaters.

The realization was followed by an outburst from Diana. She stormed Marc's place like a tornado: smashed up and burned their photographs, threw the rusty-blue Russ bear in the bin, gave Marc's arms and face a bit of a beating, and then, finally, gave him the finger.

A month after that, they kissed and made-up, and now they're together again. When Marc and I go out to drink, we pass by Diana's place, Marc checking up on her (and her checking up on him, most probably).

And as we drive down to the pub, Marc's mobile rings. Of course, I'd be the one answering it, as he'd be behind the wheel, driving us from Diana's house to the nearest beerhouse.

Most of the time, it's a text message. Sometimes, it's a phonecall.

And it's always from Odie. Well, at least, everytime I answer it.

-Juli
