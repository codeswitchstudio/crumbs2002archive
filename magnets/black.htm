when i was small, i had a fondness for buttons. not those things that fasten your clothes, but those shiny, colored, and (usually) round things that you fasten with safety pins at the back.

you could say i was fasten-ated. hahah (sorry, i couldn't resist)

anyway, i collected a bunch of those. there was the peace sign button (how Sixties) a yellow smiley one (how Seventies) and one with concentric circles in rainbow colors from a young aunt of mine. There were buttons with product logos like the BMW "pizza" and buttons with catchphrases ("when in doubt, shut up") and political slogans ("marcos, resign; ninoy di ka nag-iisa"). i even got a bunch of little traffic signs ("stop!" "yield")
that came free inside bags of jack and jill cheesecurls. my favorite pin, however, was a museum souvenir with the mona lisa wearing curlers in her hair.

when i was small, i also wondered why some people fastened these small black plastic rectangles on their shirts. intrigued by the object, i even asked a classmate of mine to trade hers with one of my cheesecurls freebies. she looked at me like i was some kind of freak. no wonder, i thought after she explained to me that those black things meant that someone in her family just died.

a modern sack cloth and ashes. i don't see those anymore.

when my own lolo died, we didn't fasten any black rectangles. incidentally that lolo of mine left this collection of silvery Russian pins engraved with Lenin's head, stars, and intriguing cyrillic inscriptions. cleaning out my
closet, i found them last week stuck on an old acid-washed denim jacket.

only one was in good shape. the rest had turned to rust.

-Circe
