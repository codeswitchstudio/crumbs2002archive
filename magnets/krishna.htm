"Krishna. I'm changing my name to Krishna….." She speaks with her head up, as if addressing the gray clouds hovering above us.

"So you're into Vedic literature now?" I start, and as usual, she pays me no heed.

"Sri Krishna, the all-attractive, original being!" She continues in this voice, which is even more theatrical than her usual. I wouldn't be surprised if the thunder rumbled on cue."I don't see what's wrong with 'Gin', Gin." I note, trying to keep up with her as we make our way through this minefield of murky puddles and sensitive issues that go beyond the small talk of newly-discovered alliances.

I check my shoes to make sure the water doesn't seep in. Yesterday's fresh asphalt (I still remember its smell and her boot-print on that steaming hot patch of bulldozed black) now reminds me of old coffee dregs floating in today's fresh potholes.

"My father gave me my name." She says, wrinkling her nose. "He comes up with Castor and Pollux for the twin troglodytes. To me, he gives his own ma's name. Virginia. Now, how creative is that?"

Without warning, she jumps right into one of the rain-riddled craters. "Lola's name. He's into recycling, alright . Recycling and Segregation. Mister environmentalist politician yada-yada." She scoffs.

There is not much love there, as she talks in her trademark animated way. Her father held a fork against her head when she crawled into the house at 4 this morning. It hadn't bled, her scalp, but I can imagine the mark the prong made on the skin beneath her soft shorn hair. -Circe
