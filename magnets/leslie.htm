The actor Leslie Nielsen slipped into a coma a couple of days ago. That was one of the last things I read in the papers.

You have anything to declare? The guy in uniform said. I shook my head.

I realized before, that the world becomes smaller as you grow old. The process is vastly exaggerated in the afterlife. Well in mine, anyway. I was still reeling, seeing all these familiar faces in the line ahead of me. I figured, it doesn't matter if our lives never intersected, the fact is, a few people who I saw in my lifetime (who, I guess, had croaked at approximately the same time frame) were in the same queue. An amusing irony. Famous stars in your old real life have bit parts in your afterlife.

It appears though, that not everyone here could see me. Only one familiar face actually acknowledged my presence a while ago. On my way to deposit my Emotional Baggage, I spotted the neighborhood street kid, the glue-sniffing waif. I tried to follow her but the crowd was too thick. I saw her head for the Lost and Found Section (to regain her lost childhood, most likely).

Standing in line, waiting for forever isn't all that boring. Leslie Nielsen, the silver-haired comedian was three people ahead of me. He was arguing with the officer about his Expected Time of Departure.

Suddenly a guy at the very end of the long, long line excitedly called out his name. It was Manoling Morato.

Leslie ran to him like they were lovers separated by extraordinary yet unavoidable circumstances. Maybe they were. -Circe
