I met her on a Friday.

"There's a voice in my left ear," she told me. "I'm in a dark room, it's nearly four o'clock in the morning, I have my eyes closed, and there's a voice inside my left ear."

"I love you," I whispered to the phone. "No, you don't." she whispered back. "Yes, I do." I whispered again.

I don't know why we were whispering, really. We were both inside a dark room, in bed, phone speakers to our ears, receivers inches from our mouths, eyes closed... physically, we were miles apart. Not too far away, but still, miles apart.

"I have every reason to love you," I whispered again, but now a bit louder (but it was still a whisper). "and none to not love you. You're a brilliant girl, Saffron. You make me laugh, you make me cry... " I let it linger a bit, waiting for her reaction. I could hear her motorized fan humming in the background, and her quick breaths.

"And you're a pretty girl, too. Everytime I see you, I fight the urge to kiss you. Gentle-soft curves, I want to run my hands over your cheeks, your eyes, your nose... tracing the shape and contour of your face... and you know Cohen."

She laughed at that, her throaty laugh.

"I may sound like I'm ripping Murakami off, but you're the 100% Perfect Girl for me. My Dream Girl. Everything I've ever dreamed of, hoped for, wished for... you're a Dream, Saffron."

"And I'm glad I met you."

A pregnant pause. Pauses in our conversations were getting more and more frequent as the days went by, for some reason or the other.

"I don't quite know how to react to that, Sid." she told me, her voice serious.

"You don't have to react, Saffron." I replied.

Another pause. From her end of the line, I could hear the rain starting. "It's raining." she said, wonder in her voice. I listen to it.

"Yes. Yes it is."
