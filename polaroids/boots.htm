BOOTS

This one has my Uncle Wowie in it. This is one of those really nice candid pictures. You know the sort I'm talking about? This one has him trimming his hair with a pair of black scissors. He's seated on one of our wrought-iron chairs, peering into a mirror. This was taken at around April, I think, in my grandparent's garage.

My Uncle Wowie used to go to the city library to borrow some books. He'd go out of his way to pass by our place to show me his latest finds: old National Geographic Anthologies, a huge book on whales, this wide book with paintings in them. Every month he'd have three or four new ones. He used to wear these really nice-looking leather cowboy boots, whose feet tapered to the front, forming a sharp point. It was knee-high, and it made this tip-tap-topping noise when he walked. It was so loud, you could hear it from thirty feet away.

Whenever I heard his boots, I ran outside the house and waited for him in the yard, eager to see and read his new books from the library.

This photo was taken about a month or so before he died. We cremated his body with his boots. There was this big pile of ashes inside the oven. I remember thinking about how much of those ashes were from my uncle and how much were from his boots. It didn't matter, I thought, as the boots were him, too. - Adam
