Sun & Shadow

contributed by GR_M

The next polaroid showed a patch of afternoon sky, the one dark enough that appears exactly fifty six minutes before sunset when one walks amid the highrises of Quezon City. The sun hid within a bank of horizontally-oriented clouds that day. The sun was the color of lemons and as we looked up, it seemed that a dark tower rose and fell in spite of the sun's dying rays. The tower was a shadow cast as if by the empire state itself but only in the Philippines. From its base out came the sun's rays, outlining its exquisite darkness. We stared in awe, me and my friends, distracted by the abject beauty of it all. They stared at it in entertainment, the yellow flush appealing tho their sense of wonder. I stared at it, entranced by the darkness and how sharply it overtook the light, even though it wasn't time yet...
