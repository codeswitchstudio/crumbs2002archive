THE SNORER

I slept with him twice. Really just slept, in the literal sense. The first time we were roomies at this ramshackle inn in the highlands. He was there for less than 24 hours and he bunked with me out of, well, homophobia for this guy in our group that he was supposed to bunk with. We were laughing at some lame joke, having had too much weed.

The second time was in his room. There was a party and I vaguely remember why I ended up lying there on his left arm. There was a dry kiss on lips parched from too much beer. Then no more.

While he slept soundly, I heard the horn blares of early-morning buses on the thoroughfare just around the corner and pretended we were in some cliché city that never sleeps.

I'd pretend it was Paris and outside his window was the Eiffel tower instead of the local network transmitter with its ultraviolet glow, waiting to fill airwaves with the signals of high-rating and money raking cheesy gossip fare and variety show inanities.

Soon the dawn would break and the metro train would rumble back to life, and his folks would arrive. I tiptoed to the washroom downstairs, brushed my teeth, went back to his room and slipped on my socks, and laced up my shoes.

I pinched his nose and ruffled his hair and he woke up and said he'd walk me to the bus stop.

-Alice
