Flying Saucer

Another picture from the 80s. RJFM used to have their radio station in the Old Manila district, along the University Belt. The booth was way up in the air, actually, shaped like a flying saucer. A bit like the Space Needle. You could only get up the booth through a spiral staircase winding from the rooftop of the building, into the flying saucer booth.

This picture was taken inside the flying saucer booth. My dad is wearing his red corduroy trousers (that I still have in the closet), green military coat (unbuttoned), with that faded-green shirt that says PINOY ROCK in thin black letters (last time I saw that shirt, I think I was nine), and a black beret. The photo is a bit cropped on the sides, so we can't see his DMs.

My mum is wearing her pink Debbie Harry-esque coat (the sort that balloons on the arms), black knee-length tights (I think. Can't tell from here), and her black high-cut cowboy boots. Her hair is short here, boyish and blonde, almost as short as my dad's. On her left hand is a fag, blurred as she was playing with it. You can see her fingernails are painted sky-blue.

Last time I was near the flying saucer booth, I was with my brother, late last year. He pointed it out to me inside the jeep. It now had mobile aerials growing around it, like fungi on a twig. - Adam
