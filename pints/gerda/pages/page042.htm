She tenses up a bit and knocks over her half-empty bottle of beer. I take several napkins from the table next to us, making them soak up the spilt beer on the table.

"I have to go home, Sid, I'm sorry," she tells me while opening her bag. "My son is waiting for me." I was about to pull out my wallet when I suddenly realize what she just said.

You have a son? I ask her in my head.
