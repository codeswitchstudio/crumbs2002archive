Several boxes of books arrived the other Monday.Mostly old stuff from my parents' home. They were cleaning-out the place, decided to run an inventory check of sorts on everything inside the house. They even made lists on what to give out, what to pass on to friends and family.

 

 

Wide books, they were. Two were Belgian BDs: Herge's 'Tintin and the Prisoners of the Sun' and 'the Crab with the Golden Claws'. BDs from my childhood. I put them on top of the stack beside me, so I could show it to Saffron later.

The third book, the one at the bottom of the box, was the Illustrated Kama Sutra (Sir Richard Burton translation)>>>

Vogue and Tatler and whatever Brit fashion rag there was (my ma's); fantasy paperbacks like the 'Thomas Covenant' series, some early Moorcock, and some sci-fi, too. There's the occasional Japanese novel, some Anais Nin, some history books… a nice collection, all told. And all mine.

Thursday that same week, even after Saffron dropped by to help me with the books, I was still sifting through the stacks, and I actually ran out of bookshelves.

The interior designers inside the two of us got to thinking, and by Saturday, we turned the living room into a library of sorts: rather large Indian cushions in

the middle of the carpet for the guests (or whoever) to sit on, with the books running around three sides of the carpet, stacked by the three walls in the living room, so the reader, if seated on the cushions, is encircled by books of every manner and size. Nice and cozy, actually.

By the next Monday, the living room cushions became my unofficial bedspread, as I always dozed off on them every night while reading books.

Wednesday, another box came. A smaller one, this time, and when I opened it, I found that it only had three books inside.

Being the writer in the family, I got the books. Heavy bastards, they were: old issues of National Geographic, reaching as far back as 1951 (my dad's); comprehensive 80s - 90s collection of
