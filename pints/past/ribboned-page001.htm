Was looking through my files the other night. My editor called me up early that day, asking me to come up with something for the paper, fast. Pretty slow month for me, actually. Spent a good part of it mostly drunk out of my mind. Moorcock wrote most of his sword-and-sorcery books with bottles-worth of Jack Daniel's in his stomach, forced out two hundred pages of stories about albinos with enchanted swords, just to pay the monthly bills. Never did have the stomach for that sort of thing. Rush jobs, I mean.

Hemingway used to do that, too. And look where it got him. Drinking, I mean.

Was looking through my files the other night, looking for an old short I wrote five years back. Story was about this group of astronauts finding something really weird in an ice planet, a yellow-colored house in the middle of a field of dandelions. Wrote it in this experimental form, the bastard child of screenplay and novel forms. I was happy with it, but I decided it was too weird for publication. Eventually used the same device in THE ANCHOR THROUGH THE CATTLE. Was in the Post's bestseller's list for around a month or two. Something like that.

What made me decide to dig up this piece of private literature? My editor pretty much has me by my nads. And she has very sharp fingers. I'll tell you about that story at another time, perhaps.
