The sun was behind her, glaring and golden, showering us with her light. Like a halo, it stayed around her head, playing tricks with Isabel's red hair. Isabel's face had a healthy sort of paleness to it, her lips red, sparkling. It must've tasted like cherry Coke, or something similarly sweet.
The vision lasted for about three seconds, tops.
But pictures speak a thousand words, I heard once or twice in the past. I figured, maybe, it works the other way around, too. A thousand words draw a picture.
Fuck Economics, I remember thinking (hey, I was younger). I need to write.
I cursed silently, hating myself for not having a camera. A moment in time, I thought. Gone forever, now. My head started working, right then and there. I wanted to keep that picture in my head so bad, wanted to capture that moment forever. It was one of those Kodak moments you see in those old TV adverts, the Kodak moments
you never really stumble on. When you do, the camera's not with you.
