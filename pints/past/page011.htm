Grabbed a notebook from my bag, didn't even know what notebook that was (I think it was my History notebook), wrote down a small diary-sort of passage. One paragraph. It said:

"Tuesday morning. Stuart (my teacher) babbling on about Economics, when a Goddess came down from Olympus and knocked on our classroom door. The sun was her halo, her robes sparkling, golden. Face was as pale as moonlight, and just as magical. Smile was as sweet as cherries.

"Never forget."

Amateurish, I know. But I was sixteen, then. And it was a start. Isabel was my reason. Decided to spend the rest of my life writing. I admit that I forgot about Isabel. Never even thanked her, come to think of it. I write because of her.
Funny thought, that is. How a person can change your life so effectively, with only three seconds, without even knowing it. Funny.
Eventually got myself to looking for the sci-fi short, the one about the astronauts and the yellow house. Passed it to my editor the next day, after scribbling some cryptic passages on the margins, telling her about maybe writing an introduction for it.
