All the names were still there. The white paper color was slowly turning into yellow, but the names were still legible. Gerard, Jorge, Dennis, Lawrence, Michael… the names go on. Tier after tier it went. Boys, and then girls, and then boys again.
Then I stopped.
Isabel. I remember her.
I checked the photograph, looking for a face to go with the name. Can't find her. Checked the back, alternated between reading the names and checking the faces in the front, to see which name came with the face.
Isabel (second tier, sixth from left). Saw her. Can't see her face, though. She was one of those water-damaged faces. Damn.
Why was that name so special? I asked myself. Ex-girlfriend? Most hated enemy? First love, maybe? You never forget your first love, right? No, not my first love. That was someone else.
Isabel, Isabel. I repeat the name in an endless reel in my head, trying to bait the memory out of the back of my head, to the front.
Red hair, French bob, knobby nose, mousy look, bit of a buck toothed girl. Images flash in my head like a silent movie trailer.
Then I remember her. Isabel. She was the reason why I decided to become a writer.
