Years later, an artist friend of mine drew up this experimental painting of his, mixed-media. It was a picture of a girl transforming into a goddess. He was an amateur artist back in those days, was one of the starving ones, too. Anyway, he insisted on showing the painting to me. Being the great guy that I was, I agreed to see it. Probably get a story out of it, so, why not? I was doing this experiment, maybe every writer does this, but I was trying to write stories about anything that struck my fancy. I know Chesterton used to do it, writing essays about a brown paper bag, about the contents of his pockets, stuff like that. I read it somewhere, and thought it was clever and all, and probably nice practice for a young writer.
I wrote some about a homeless man that I used to see in the Park, about Saffron, wrote one about my boss, one about my brother and his nocturnal activities.
Anyway, this particular painting (MY GIRL, mixed media on canvas, 1992, private collection), the artist told me, was about how some girls turn into goddesses, even for but a second. It's been known to happen, he told me. Back in that classroom, when the door opened, I saw a goddess.
