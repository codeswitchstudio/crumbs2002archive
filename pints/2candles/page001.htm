I WAS UP ALL NIGHT last Saturday, drinking with a few friends of mine. It was Gerard's birthday, and we were drinking gin as it was the only thing in his cabinet, and everyone in the room was either too broke or too tight-arsed to spend money on drinks. The thing was, gin always did make me a bit sick in the stomach (and in the head, a few moments after).

Late Sunday morning, and my head is in the toilet when the 'phone rang. I let it ring for a while, the siren a bit louder in my head, reverberating. I reach for the 'phone on the sixth or seventh ring.

Hello? I groan into the receiver. My mouth tastes like something died in it.

"Sid? It's Saffron. What took you so long to answer the bloody 'phone?" Saffron asks me through the 'phoneline. I was in the toilet, I tell her. Was out drinking with Gerard and Marc and…

"Have you seen today's newspaper, yet? You still get the papers, right?" she asks me, interrupting my thread of thought. Uh-huh. Too hung-over to argue, I walk to the door and look for that day's papers. I find it outside the apartment door.

Still held together by rubber band, I pick it up and plop on down the couch to read it. What am I supposed to see? I ask Saffron. "Is it the Inquirer?" she asks me. Yeah, it is. "Front page, top-left hand side corner, small box beside the title."
