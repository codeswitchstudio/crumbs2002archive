Hello, I answered back. I'm looking for someone named Saffron… "Speaking. Who's this?" she asked me playfully. Too playfully, I thought. She must've known about me. Expecting my call, even. This is Siddharta. Elvin and Rhoda's friend. "Ah." she answered. I saw her smile through the 'phone.

 

"They were kidnapped about a week ago, while they were on their way to school." Saffron tells me, sobbing. I'm still in shock, the reality of it all not yet kicking in.

Any witnesses? I ask. "There were a few, but all they said was that there was this blue… fuck, what's the point, Sid? They're dead. Dead in Cavite. The Inquirer said they were the sixth and seventh victims of kidnapping this year. They reduced them into a statistic, into a fucking group of numbers and graphs. A fucking statistic. Elvin and Rhoda. Dead in Cavite."

Are you home, Saffron? Where are you? I ask. There is a pause, about a second or so of stifled sobs. "Yeah… yeah, I'm home."

I'll nip on down there a bit, okay, Saffron? I… I need someone to talk to… I left the sentence hanging. She picks it up. "… okay. I won't go anywhere. Come. Now. I need to talk, too."
