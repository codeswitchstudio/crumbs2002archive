I scan the front page. A small article? I ask her.

"Uh-huh. With a lengthier write-up in page A-16." I stand up and turn on the player. 'I'm in love with a certain girl, she's a simple person, not as young as she looks, but whoever is in this day and age, in this day and age, yeah…' the radio whispers in mono in Stuart Murdoch's voice. I stumble back towards the couch.

"Found it, yet?" Saffron asks me, and I notice that her voice is a bit throatier than usual. Like she was crying, or just finished crying. That sort of voice. I nod. Reading it, now, I tell her.

STUDENTS FOUND DEAD IN CAVITE, it said in title blurb bold letters. I read on. 'Two UPLB students were found dead in a small town in Cavite, not two kilometers apart. The students were reportedly kidnapped the week before, but were never reported to the police, in fear of the victims' welfare.

'The victims were Elvin Allan Chua and Maria Rhoda Dolatre, both 28, Journalism students from UPLB…'

I stop reading. Elvin Allan Chua. Maria Rhoda Dolatre. UPLB. In my head, I make the connection.

Elvin and Rhoda? I ask Saffron. "Uh-huh. Elvin and Rhoda." she answers me, her voice breaking. My god.
