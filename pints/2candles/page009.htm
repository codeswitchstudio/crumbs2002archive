"When did we last see them?" Saffron asks, the question addressed more to herself than to me. "Was it last year? They invited us up to Baguio, remember?

They were taking care of this place there, and it was empty for the weekend… " I nod. I remember. Rhoda and I fooled around a bit, that weekend.

"You should remember, as you and Rhoda fooled around a bit that weekend." What? I ask her, a bit shocked. How… how did you know? She laughs, hand over her mouth. "You thought I didn't know? How could I NOT know? We were watching the telly, Elvin and I, and we couldn't help but hear the two of you rut like animals on the second floor. I mean, we could hear bangings and footsteps and grunts. Elvin and I were giggling

like high school girls, man." Bangings? Footsteps? Grunts? She laughs again. "I didn't know who to feel sorry for, her or you." she says with a smile, and the two of us laugh together.

She stands up and walks to her radio and turns it on. Astrud comes out of the stereo speakers. "Once, I loved…" Saffron sings with it a bit, about five seconds or so before she breaks out in tears. I walk to her and embrace her from behind. "No one deserved to die that way, Sid. Especially not Elvin and Rhoda. Especially not Elvin and Rhoda… god…" she says between sobs. I feel tears running down my cheeks. She turns around, facing me, and we stare into each other's eyes. Her nose and eyes are pink from crying, three pink spots in a wonderfully pale face. I kiss her on her forehead, on her eyes, on her nose, and on both cheeks.
