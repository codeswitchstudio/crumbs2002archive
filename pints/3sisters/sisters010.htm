"Oh, I just didn't feel like wearing them tonight. You know?" I nodded, even when I had no idea what she was talking about. She puts her mobile back in her bag. I follow her hands with my eyes, and see several rolls of sacheted condoms. Somewhere inside me, I smile. How very Robin, I think again. Ever the girl scout.

I glance at my watch. 11:10. Still no Saffron. I look at the glasses on the table. Five glasses. Five pints. Three are empty, of which two are mine. On my third pint, and still no Saffron.

 

She laughs and pulls out her mobile, a thin one, yellow-colored, looks a bit like a Tricorder. How very Robin, I tell myself.

 

When beepers came out, she had hers in less than a month. She was like that. I give her my number, Robin pressing the numbers on the keypad. I ask her about her contact lenses.
