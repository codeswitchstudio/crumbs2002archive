Right now it was night, and I was waiting for Saffron. It was that time of the year again. Annual drink-up. She called me up the day before, to remind me. Wasn't there, but the answerphone was.

"Sid," she starts. Her voice was still that way, youthful and girly. "It's Saffron. Checked the calendar lately? It's March, man. Nearly your birthday, and you-know- what-else. Call me? New number: 6116669. Trippy number, don't you think? Anyway, hear you later."

Wrote the number down and called her up. She wasn't there, but her answerphone was.

I was there by 10, and took our regular table beside the door, by the big glass window overlooking the street. At mornings, I go there and just stare outside, making up stories in my head about the people I see. It's good practice for a writer, a friend of mine told me when I told her about what I was doing. I made this screenplay for Richard the other month, something good for 30 minutes. A bit of a drama-romance, with the characters' lives intersecting here and there. Pretty complex, is what I think. Anyway, what I used for characters were some of the characters (and stories) that I got from staring outside the window. Like what my friend said. Good practice.
