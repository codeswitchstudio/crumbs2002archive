I look at my watch. Quarter to 11. Still waiting for Saffron.

A pair of hands from behind cover my eyes. I freeze by reflex. Must be the fear of sudden darkness in our hearts, the back of my mind told me. After a second, I recover, feeling for the hands.

Soft and silky and warm. Thin fingers, rather long nails. A wristwatch, tightly strapped on the left wrist. Thin wrists. I feel for hair, but find none. Feminine, I thought. And the smell was familiar. Saffron? I thought. Not sure. Couldn't place where I know the hands from.

I definitely hit the spot, there. I can make it work, I tell him. I scribble words on my notebook, ideas, devices, some dialogue, character ideas. With two pints of beer in our stomachs, Richard and I form a movie idea good for a lengthy feature.

I'll send out an APB on you when I finish writing this, I tell him. He nods and leaves the table, automatically gravitating towards the telly. Kubrick's LOLITA is on. Someone brought a tape, I thought. No local station shows that sort of movie here.
