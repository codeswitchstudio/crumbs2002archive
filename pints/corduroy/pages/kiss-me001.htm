I walk to the kitchen and turn on the oven, reheating another box of pizza.

I pull out two bottles of beer from the fridge. Saffron walks in wearing my KISS ME shirt and my old pajama bottoms. I smile and point to the bottle of beer. Yours, I say.

"Thanks," she tells me. She raises the bottle to her lips, drinking. Her eyebrows then rise, as if she remembers something. "You still have this shirt," she tells me, pulling at the KISS ME shirt. Yeah, I answer back. Oh yeah. Saffron bought me that shirt from the same bargain place where we got her corduroy jacket. And you still have the jacket, I tell her. "Uh-huh," she tells me through the bottle. There's pizza in the oven, I tell her. I'm reheating it. Molten cheese and all.

"Mmm, molten cheese! You know the shape of my heart, Sid!" she tells me in her bright, sparkling voice. Of course I do, I tell her, opening the oven door. I spent about a year and a half of my life beside it, I say with a smile. "Silly boy!" she says, pinching my nose. The oven pings. I open it and pull out the pizza. As soon as I open the oven door, Saffron takes a generous whiff of the hot air drifting from the inside. "Let's eat it in the living room!" Saffron tells me. I nod, and she runs off to the living room, bottle of beer in hand, wearing my KISS ME shirt and pajama bottoms. The smell of molten cheese drifts off behind her.
