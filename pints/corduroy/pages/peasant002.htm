I wake up the next morning, the sun finally up and out, the rain gone.

I glance at my wristwatch, telling me it's one o'clock in the afternoon. I rub my face with my tee-shirt, and then walk to the bed to check on Saffron. She's not there. In her place I find a pile of clothes, my KISS ME tee-shirt and old pajama bottoms, and a gift-wrapped package. The wrapping she used was yesterday's newspaper. I pick up the package and open it. Inside is a CD. The cover has a photograph of two girls on it, cleverly taken. At first glance, you'd think it was a shot of a girl looking at a mirror, when it's actually a shot of two girls staring at each other, possibly twins. It's Belle & Sebastian's FOLD YOUR HANDS CHILD, YOU WALK LIKE A PEASANT. With the disc is a note folded in half, with my name written on it. It was written with the girly childish scribble of Saffron's handwriting. I fold it open.

"Sid," Saffron tells me through the note. "Last night was fun. Sorry about your party, but it did give us time to catch up on things. Thought about waking you up, to talk to you personally, but you were sleeping so soundly, like a baby, I didn't have the heart to wake you up. Bought you the new B&S disc!! Play track number five for me. Anyway, again: Happy birthday. Call me?" and she ends it with a red kiss mark on the side, and below it she wrote her name: "Saffron".

I walk to the stereo and insert the disc. Program it to track number five, and then press play. A melancholy song comes out of the speakers.

"Don't leave the light on, baby… I'll see you sometime, maybe… don't leave the light on, baby…"
