The sound of galloping horses woke me up.

Rain was falling all over the city in raindrops as big as buckets, the roof was rattling like stallions were trampling on it. Found my wristwatch: eight o'clock, it told me. Eight in the PM, and it was raining. I stand up and walk to the 'phone, thinking about canceling the party, and was greeted by the answerphone's blinking red light. Someone left a message, my still sleepy head told me. Why, yes, indeed, I answered back. Someone left a message. I pressed the answerphone rewind button. Stop. Play. Listen.

"Hey, Sid, it's Gerard," the answerphone told me in Gerard's voice. "Sorry I can't go to the party, man. Rain and all, fucked up our driveway like anything. The flood's up to my knees, man. Anyway, maybe next week? A drink-up? Okay. Oh yeah, happy birthday!"

*beep*

"Sid, love, it's Robin here. Sorry we can't make it to your place. Rain's just too hard right now. Maybe when it clears up? Anyway, I'll call you up later. Happy birthday!"

*beep*

"Your bro, Sid. Rain's too awful for me to commute down to your place. Sorry. I know I promised you stuff, take you down to the zoo or something. Maybe next week? With Sean and Nicholas, maybe? Happy birthday, okay? Yeah."

*beep*

"'Ey shit-for-brains birthday boy! Flood down here's up to me nostrils, man. The river's swelling like hell, and David needs help with the rotten plumbing job here. Rain's been puking sewage out of every drainage in the house, it already smells like Farmer's Market in the bumper end, man. it has to be smelt to be belie--"

And it went on and on, message after message. Keywords were "sorry" and "rain" and "birthday". Looking out my window, I was actually pretty relieved. It was a bit like a squall outside. Quite the sight, I thought. Went to the kitchen, took out one box of pizza, went to the fridge and pulled out a bottle of beer. Fuck those guys, I thought. And fuck the rain while I'm at it. I'll enjoy the night by myself, and with bloody DREAMS on the VCR.
