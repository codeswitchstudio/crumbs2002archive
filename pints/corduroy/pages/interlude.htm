"So, how was your year being a twenty-eight year old?" she asks me.

Nice question. I shrug, saying Not too bad, I think. I mean, I survived another year. Barely got through the better part of it, running away from rabid editors and drunken nymphos. Lost some friends, made some new ones. Earned some money, lost most of it. Lost weight. Lost some books, too. But I found some things that I've been looking for for years, now. Like I said, not too bad. Now, i'm twenty-nine.
She looks at me keenly, her brows bunching up. Not a fold of skin appears. Saffron is three years older than me, but looked five years younger. Fooled a lot of people, actually. It even fooled me, when I first met her.
