And then the dryer tells us Saffron's clothes are dry.

The two of us try to stand up and walk to the dryer. Saffron pulls out her clothes from the machine, and straightens them out. She smiles approvingly, eyeing her clothes. She looks back at me with a smile, her eyes smiling with her. I remember that, I think to myself. "They're dry," she tells me, stating the obvious. I glance at my wristwatch, and it says eleven-thirty. You still want to go home? I ask her. She asks me what time it is, and I tell her. She frowns, and touches her nose, feeling it with her hand.

Then, she turns to me and asks "Can I sleep over? I'm too drunk to go home…" I nod, telling her Of course. You can use my bed. I can sleep on the sofa.

"How about two more bottles before we go to sleep?" she asks me. I shrug my shoulders. Why not? We walk to the fridge and pull out two more bottles of beer, ice-cold, colder than our previous bottles. I pass her a bottle, and we walk back to the living room. She walks to the stereo and changes the disc. Pressed play, and Stuart and Co. starts singing in the stereo. 'Judy and the Dream of Horses'. A sweet song. We plop on down the sofa.

I pull my head back, and the room moves with me. I'm drunk, I tell Saffron. She laughs, and says "Me too. How much beer did we drink, anyway?" she asks me. I look at the table and concentrate, counting the empty bottles. I think I drank six, and you had five. This is my seventh, and that's your sixth. She nods and smiles. She stares at her bottle, half-empty.

"Mine's half-empty." Saffron tells me.

No it's not, I tell her. It's half-full. She laughs out loud. I could hear her thoughts saying "Silly boy!!"

"How about a toast, Sid? I mean, it is your birthday, and the two of us are drinking… perfect set-up for a bloody great toast."

A toast? A toast to what? I ask. She smiles.

"I already thought-up of one, actually" she says.

Out with it, then! We lift our beer and hold them in mid-air, an inch apart. She clears her throat.

"To rusty aerials, and… what was the other one?"

Uh… absent stars? I suggest.

"Brilliant!! God, I'm pissed-face!! To rusty aerials, and absent stars," she says with a satisfied smile. A clink and two swigs.

And then she drifts off to sleep with a smile on her face.

I push her gently, trying to wake her up. No success. I stand up, my knees a bit wobbly, and I move towards her. I carry her (she's so light! Or maybe it's that temporary strength that beer gives us) to the bed, and I cover her with the blankets. She curls by reflex, and mumbles something in her sleep. "Happy birthday, Sid." And then she smiles.

I walk to the sofa (my bed for the night!) and I finish my bottle, and then Saffron's. I sleep while sitting up.
