I forgot to stop the player, and the credits were already rolling when we got to the sofa.

She recognizes the scene, "Dreams?" she asks me. I nod. Yeah, it is.

"Wonderful movie, wasn't it? Sad and beautiful in all the right places." Yeah, I say, putting the pizza on top of the table. Saffron looks around the place, her eyes jumping from

one corner of the room to the other. She finds the stereo.

"Does that thing still play?" she asks me, pointing at the stereo. I nod. Yeah, cassette and CD. Look for something nice, I tell her. She walks to the stack of CDs beside the player, shuffling through them with ease, like they were cards. Her hands are long, as she was in the rather thin and tall side. She finds one she likes, pushes a button to open the CD tray, and puts the CD in. Press play, and I hear 'The State I Am In'.

"I was surprised, I was happy for a day, in 1975… I was puzzled by a dream, stayed with me all day, in 1995…" she turns around slowly and sings with the CD, using the beer bottle as a faux-microphone, Sufi-dancing with the tune. I smile and drink and remind her: Oi, Tigermilk. You bought me that disc.

"You remember?" she asks me.

Of course I do, I tell her.

I was looking for worthwhile records back then: BIS, SUPER FURRY ANIMALS, that WEEZER Pinkerton album… the two of us were shuffling through bargain shelves of CDs in a place called Chapel. Shuffling through CDs of artists with names like SCHOOL BOYS, PINK TRIANGLE, ELEPHANT BABIES, and several anthologies and soundtracks. Saffron pulled out a CD from the shelf, walked to the counter, and paid for it.

"Do you like indie?" she asked me.

Do I like indie? I love indie! I answered. She smiled.

"Hold out your hand," she said. I held it out and she put the CD on top of it, telling me "For you. Try this one. Heard it back when I was in Glasgow, I think you'll love it." When I got home, went straight to the player and pressed play. Flew up to heaven with the album, never really came back.

"Oh love of mine, would you condescend to help me, 'cause I'm stupid and blind… oh, desperation is the Devil's work, it is the folly of a boy's empty mind…"

She settles back down beside me. I notice her beer is still pretty full, and I tell her that. "Hold your horses, cowboy," she tells me. "You don't want me puking all over your carpets. Take my word for it: it stinks!" I smile and she smiles, and we both drink our beer.
