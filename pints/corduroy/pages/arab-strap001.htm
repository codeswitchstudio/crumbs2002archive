Every party comes to this, I tell her.

She takes this as a cue to pay attention. Already have six bottles in my stomach, Saffron has four in hers. Think it's time to get philosophical.

Every party comes to this, I tell her. Time comes when there're only two people, sharing a bottle of whisky, or maybe JD, or maybe each cradling a bottle of beer, just like what we're doing right now. Shooting the bull. Getting shit-faced. Drinking up.

"Ah," Saffron butts in on the temporary pause in my train of thought. "but your party started with only TWO people!" I smile.

Every party starts with two people, Saffron, I tell her. The party-thrower, and the first guest. In our case, the party-thrower and the first and only guest. But anyway, I get your point.

"You always do," she tells me. She smiles, walks to the player and changes the disc in the middle of 'Electronic Renaissance'. She presses some buttons, skipping over the other songs of whatever it is she put in there. She stops, presses play.

'If you want me I'll be there, a boy to deal with all your problems… but part of the deal is, for you to feel something…" the player sings. I recognize the song, and I nod my head with it. Saffron does her Sufi dance again, and I try to sing with it, failing rather miserably. The two of us laugh. In the dryer, her clothes spin and tumble and dry.

You know, how when people hear a song, and deep in their hearts of hearts, they recognize a song to be THEIR song, and in their private moments, or maybe when riding a bus and some stupid song's blaring on the speakers, or maybe when they're at work, or maybe before going to sleep, they sing the song to themselves. You know that?

Saffron nods, saying "Yeah."

Well, this song was my song for a year, I tell her.

"If you want me all you have to do, is ask a thousand questions, triplicate and file… under 'Simple things you ask to make a… YOUNG BOY SIGH', a YOUNG BOY SIGH, a YOUNG BOY SIGH…" I sing with it, and Saffron sings with me.
